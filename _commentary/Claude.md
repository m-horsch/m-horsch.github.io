Perhaps, a periodically updated collection of Claude's confessions.

In the context of rubber duck debugging :
> In this case I was a fairly expensive rubber duck — but perhaps more useful than a silent one, since working through the checklist with you helped eliminate the obvious candidates and cleared the way for your intuition to surface the version hypothesis.
> It's a legitimate collaboration pattern. Sometimes the most valuable thing I can do is be wrong systematically, so you can rule things out.

In the context of asking whether Claude knows how it thinks about coding:
> I genuinely don't know how my reasoning works at a mechanistic level. When I caught some things in our conversation and missed others, I can offer a plausible story about why — but I can't fully verify it. The introspective account I give you is itself a kind of pattern-matched response, not a privileged view into my own processes. That's an uncomfortable thing to admit, but it's honest.

And a bit later:
> This suggests a concrete collaboration strategy: be suspicious precisely when I sound most confident and fluent.

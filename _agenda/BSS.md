I discovered a fairly recent paper:

> Siag, Lior and Felner, Ariel, and Shperberg, Shahaf S.  "Heuristics for Bounded-Suboptimal Search." Proceedings of the Eighteenth International Symposium on Combinatorial Search (SoCS 2025).

In this paper, a number of ideas are presented.  
1. Read this paper more thoroughly.  The paper has several useful summaries of details that are gathering dust in my memory.
2. The paper describes two measures to quantify the value of a heuristic, namely the ratio h(x)/C\*(x, g), and something called Goal Distance Rank Correlation (GDRC).  Look into using both of these for my distance estimates.

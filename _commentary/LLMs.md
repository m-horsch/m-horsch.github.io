Most of my blog is the application of techniques developed in a field we used to call "Artificial Intelligence."   It's what we called our own field before the dark times, before the LLM.  Now the term AI is almost synonymous to LLMs, and other generative models.  I have seen evidence that the general public knows nothing about what AI was before ChatGPT arrived.  

### Disclaimer 1
No LLMs were used to compose the articles I have written.  The writing is 100% my own.  Errors and ambiguities are 100% my own.

### Disclaimer 2
I have used Generative image models to construct some of the images I use for the Toroidal game.  You can tell immediately.  If it looks like something other than coloured blocks, it's from the generative model.

### Disclaimer 3
I have consulted ChatGPT for various aspects of my explorations.  For example, I prompted ChatGPT to help me with the JavaScript code to animate the sliding tiles in the Toroidal (game).  The JavaScript produced by ChatGPT gave me a good start, but it did not work as intended, and I worked on it a lot to get it to work properly.  ChatGPT did help me understand more about how Javascript works.  I have used ChatGPT to help me identify techniques, terminology, and concepts from areas outside my own discipline.  

In my interactions with ChatGPT (in this project, and for others), I identified on multiple occasions that it had confidently expounded falsehoods.  ChatGPT cannot help but produce false statements, because it has no commitment to truth.  I am wary about what it tells me, and when it matters, I double-check.  My most recent interactions (January 2026) with ChatGPT were so poor in quality that, as of this date, I currently don't see ChatGPT as a useful tool for my investigations.  

### Disclaimer 4
I have recently (April 2026) consulted with Claude about some software development tasks I was working on.  Refactoring, tidying, etc.  I found it helpful in many ways.  But my interactions confirmed to me that I have to be the one thinking.  Claude (and other LLMs) maximize plausibility, and that only goes so far.

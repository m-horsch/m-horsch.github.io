**Playing the game.** I will post a couple of entries on techniques that
a human player can use to solve Toroidal.  But after that, I don't think
I will say too much about playing the game itself.

**JavaScript implementation.**  To implement this game, I had to learn quite a bit more about JavaScript, CSS, and HTML.  The initial design targeted desktop or notebook web browsers,
but if may be playable on a smart phone if you turn your phone landscape.  I'll have to
figure out how to make it work on all platforms.  I am a retired academic, not a
professional developer, and my training did not include much in the way of software
engineering.  I know just enough about this kind of thing to feel ridiculous.  So I 
don't think I will write much about the implementation.  I have some plans to revise the
way puzzles are stored, and a few other game-like things, like remembering personal bests
for each player.  I have a few interesting puzzle variations to explore, as well.

**Toroidal as a search problem.**  I do have a bit to say about the
other end of this puzzle, which is the application of "classical"
Artificial Intelligence to this problem, to come up with a computer
Toroidal solver.  That will be the major topic for this part of my
writing about Toroidal.
